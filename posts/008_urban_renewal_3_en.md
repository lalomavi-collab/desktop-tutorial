3 questions every property owner must ask before signing an urban renewal deal:

Most owners receive an offer from a developer.
Read it once.
Ask a neighbor.
Sign.

Three years later — they're still waiting for their apartment.

**Question 1: What happens if the developer faces financial difficulties mid-construction?**
What is your protection mechanism?
Is there a bank guarantee? What is its scope?
Who continues the project if the developer stumbles?

**Question 2: What qualifies as "force majeure" in the contract — and what doesn't?**
War, pandemic, permit delays —
who bears the risk when the timeline extends?
Is rental compensation contractually guaranteed?

**Question 3: Who represents you — and only you?**
The attorney the developer appoints to "handle the residents"
is not your attorney.
They represent the transaction.
Those are two fundamentally different things.

**Real representation begins before the signature.**
Not after.
