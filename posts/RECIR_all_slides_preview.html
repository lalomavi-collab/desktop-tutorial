PART 5 · RESULTS · TEMPORAL ROBUSTNESS
Temporal Generalization (2015–2023)
Risk pattern recovery holds through COVID-19 and war — empirical robustness.
R² PER YEAR · 10-FOLD NESTED CV · RANGE 0.996–1.000
If this were overfitting, fold variance would explode under crisis. It does not.
WHY THE MODEL DIDN'T COLLAPSE
Our 9-year panel data includes severe market shocks — COVID-19 and the 2022 interest-rate hikes. The HGBRg didn't collapse during these crises because of an optimized binning strategy: features are compressed into 255 discrete bins. Extreme outlier values from turbulent years fall into edge bins and lose their numerical power to distort the algorithm. This is precisely what prevents tail risk from exploding the model in chaotic markets.

PART 9 · CLARIFICATION · C·5 THREE-LAYER METHODOLOGY
Why R² = 0.9988? — Three Methodological Layers
The reported accuracy is not a single-step computation — it is the product of three sequential filters.
LAYER 1
Multicollinearity Filter
VIF < 5
Variables with high cross-correlation are removed before training. Eliminates redundant signals that artificially inflate explanatory power.
✓ Spurious correlation → controlled
LAYER 2
Feature Selection (RFE-CV)
198 → 90 features
Recursive Feature Elimination with cross-validation retains only features with genuine predictive contribution. Reduces dimensionality without information loss.
✓ Noise filtered → signal preserved
LAYER 3
Nested Cross-Validation
10 outer folds
Outer loop separates training from hyperparameter tuning. Standard k-fold leaks information via tuning; nested CV does not.
✓ Zero data leakage → honest R²
R² = 0.9988 is the outcome of three independent safeguards — not a single optimistic estimate.

Geographic Generalization — Adapting RECIR to New Jurisdictions
A structured four-step process: local data substitution, re-calibration, regulatory alignment, and re-validation.
STEP 1
Local Data Substitution
AHS → Local
Replace AHS/WDI inputs with local equivalents — Spanish Catastro, UK Land Registry, or LATAM cadastral databases. Feature mapping validated by local domain experts.
✓ Input layer adapted to local market
STEP 2
Weight Re-Calibration
HGBRg Re-Trained
Re-run HGBRg on local dataset with RFE-CV. Governance and location weights will shift to reflect local regulatory structure (e.g., planning law in Spain vs. UK). Reduces dimensionality without information loss.
✓ Risk weights reflect local market
STEP 3
Regulatory Alignment
GDPR + Local Law
Map local legal framework to RECIR indices — EU AI Act Art. 13, GDPR for data handling, and jurisdiction-specific zoning/planning statutes. Re-validate EU AI Act compliance.
✓ Framework legally compliant in new jurisdiction
Geographic generalization is a formal protocol — not a simple parameter swap. Each jurisdiction requires full re-validation (Spearman ρ against local expert panel) before deployment.
