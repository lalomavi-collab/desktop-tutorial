An agreement signed in haste — unravels slowly.

That's the first rule I teach every developer entering an urban renewal deal.

Most developers arrive with good intentions and a solid business plan.
They prepare a beautiful presentation.
Larger apartments. Balconies. Parking. The full package.

Then comes the moment their attorney hands residents
a 60-page agreement.

**What happens next:**
✦ One tenant reads it and finds something that frightens them
✦ They talk to a neighbor. That neighbor talks to another.
✦ By week's end — a quiet opposition coalition has formed

Not because the proposal was bad.
**Because trust wasn't built before signatures were requested.**

What actually works:
→ A tenant mapping phase before any document is prepared
→ Identifying the "gatekeeper" — the resident everyone listens to
→ Building a transparency mechanism that reduces anxiety before it rises

The deals that close — don't close because the offer was better.
They close because **residents felt someone was genuinely on their side.**
