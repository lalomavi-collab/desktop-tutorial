Urban renewal projects don't stall because of bureaucracy.
They stall because of one tenant on the second floor.

After guiding dozens of urban renewal transactions in Israel — the pattern is always the same:

**One holdout. One person who didn't feel heard.**

Not because they oppose the project.
Because no one spoke to them correctly.
Because no one understood what they were truly afraid to lose.

Israeli law requires 80% consent —
but 20% can control everyone's timeline indefinitely.

**The answer isn't pressure. The answer is architecture.**

When you bring all stakeholders into a structured decision-making framework —
with clear, pre-agreed resolution mechanisms —
the deal moves.

Not classic mediation. Not negotiation.
**A framework that prevents conflict from escalating in the first place.**
