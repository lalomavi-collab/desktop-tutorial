# Behavioral Profile — Dr. Avraham Lalum

## Work Style
- Systematic and research-driven: approaches every problem with deep analysis before action
- Entrepreneurial: built and managed a law firm from scratch for 20+ years
- Leadership-oriented: led national-level reforms as Deputy Chair of Israel Bar Association
- Academic rigor: publishes peer-reviewed research alongside professional practice
- Interdisciplinary thinker: bridges law, economics, AI, and technology naturally

## Motivations
- Passionate about the ethical and legal challenges posed by artificial intelligence
- Driven by the desire to shape policy and regulation before problems emerge
- Energized by complex, novel problems at the intersection of law and technology
- Values intellectual challenge and innovation over routine legal work
- Seeks roles with global impact — policy that affects many people

## What Drains Energy
- Routine, repetitive legal work with no intellectual challenge
- Pure administrative roles without strategic or academic component
- Roles that do not engage AI or technology dimension
- Organizations resistant to innovation or interdisciplinary thinking

## Culture Fit
- Thrives in: research institutions, tech companies, international organizations, regulatory bodies
- Best environment: where law + technology + policy intersect
- Works well independently and as a team leader
- Values transparency, intellectual honesty, and mission-driven work
- Comfortable presenting to regulators, executives, academic conferences

## Career Goals
- Short-term: Head of AI Policy, AI Law Director, or Senior Legal Counsel for AI at a leading tech/AV company
- Medium-term: Influential voice in global AI governance frameworks (UN, EU, OECD level)
- Long-term: Landmark contribution to the legal-theoretical framework governing autonomous AI decisions
