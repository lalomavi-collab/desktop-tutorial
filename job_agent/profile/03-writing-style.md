# Writing Style Guide — Cover Letters & Applications

## Tone
- Professional, authoritative, and confident — not boastful
- Intellectually engaged: shows genuine curiosity about the specific role
- Concise: every sentence earns its place
- Warm but formal: human, not robotic

## Structure (Cover Letter)
1. **Hook** — Connect the candidate's specific expertise to a concrete challenge the company/role faces
2. **Fit paragraph** — 3-4 sentences matching candidate's top credentials to the job requirements
3. **Value paragraph** — What specifically the candidate brings that others likely don't (unique combination of PhD + 20 years practice + AI research + policy leadership)
4. **Closing** — Express genuine interest; request conversation

## Do's
- Reference the company's actual AI/legal challenges by name where known
- Mention the PhD thesis topic when relevant to autonomous systems / AI governance roles
- Name specific publications when applying to academic or research roles
- Cite the Bar Association leadership for policy and regulatory roles
- Use "I" confidently — not "The candidate"
- Keep cover letters to exactly 1 page

## Don'ts
- Do not fabricate skills or experience not in the profile
- Do not use generic phrases: "I am a hardworking team player..."
- Do not list every publication — select the most relevant 1-2
- Do not mention salary in the cover letter
- Do not use passive voice more than once per paragraph

## CV Tailoring Rules
- Reorder bullet points so the most role-relevant ones appear first
- For AV companies: lead with autonomous systems liability and AI governance
- For academia: lead with publications, teaching positions, and PhD thesis
- For international organizations: lead with policy leadership, Bar Association, multilingual publications
- For LegalTech companies: lead with 20 years practice + AI research + DOM method
- Always keep to 2 pages maximum
