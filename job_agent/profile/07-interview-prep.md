# Interview Preparation — Dr. Avraham Lalum

## Core STAR Examples

### "Tell me about a time you shaped policy"
**S:** As Deputy Chair of the Israel Bar Association (2015–2023), the legal profession faced major regulatory changes proposed by the Ministry of Justice
**T:** Lead the Bar's legislative response to protect attorney independence while modernizing the profession
**A:** Drafted position papers, led negotiations with Ministry, coordinated 70,000-member bar's formal response
**R:** Several key provisions adopted into law; professional framework modernized while preserving attorney independence

### "Most complex legal-AI problem you solved"
**S:** Studying liability frameworks for AI-based risk models in real estate investment decisions
**T:** Create a theoretically sound framework for assigning liability when an AI makes a recommendation that causes financial harm
**A:** Applied Law & Economics tools, explainable AI analysis, and comparative regulation to build a novel multi-dimensional liability model
**R:** Published in *Mathematics* (Scopus/JCR, 2025); cited in academic and policy discussions on AI liability

### "Leading through ambiguity"
**S:** Building Lalum & Co. from scratch in 2004 with no existing client base
**T:** Establish credibility in commercial and civil law while developing unique expertise
**A:** Combined rigorous legal practice with academic engagement; developed DOM (Decision-Oriented Mediation) method to differentiate
**R:** 20+ year practice with Israeli and international clients; became recognized expert and author

### "Cross-disciplinary collaboration"
**S:** Research project with engineers on AI-based object recognition for real estate
**T:** Bridge legal/economic analysis with deep learning technical work
**A:** Led the legal and economic framework design; collaborated closely with ML engineers on model interpretability
**R:** Published in SN Business & Economics (Springer, 2024)

## Questions to Ask Employers
- How does your legal team currently engage with the AI product teams?
- What are the 2-3 biggest open regulatory questions you're navigating right now?
- How does this role engage with regulators (NHTSA, EU AI Act, etc.)?
- What does success look like in 12 months for this role?
- Is there room to publish or present academic work on topics relevant to the company's challenges?

## Key Differentiators to Emphasize
1. Only candidate who combines: 20+ years legal practice + PhD research in AI + actual published AI research
2. Has led both at executive level (Bar Association) and as academic researcher simultaneously
3. Deep autonomous systems focus — not just "tech law" generalist
4. Built methodology (DOM) — can build legal frameworks from scratch, not just apply existing ones
